I'm a business analytics master's student at Cal Poly SLO with a BS in Statistical Data Science from UC Davis, where I spent four years competing as a Division I soccer athlete. Data and sport have been intertwined for me for a long time.

My goal is to work in the sports, athletic wear, outdoor, or health industry, applying my analytical skills to problems that genuinely matter to athletes and active people. I'm drawn to roles where consumer behavior, product engagement, and performance data all intersect, whether that's at a global athletic brand, an outdoor retailer, or a national park or conservation organization.

My academic focus covers marketing analytics, financial analytics, and experimental design, with hands-on experience in predictive modeling, cohort analysis, customer segmentation, and data storytelling across technical and non-technical audiences.

Outside of work and school, I train a lot. Running, lifting, and sculpt classes are regular parts of my week. I also love getting outdoors, whether that's hiking, camping, or photographing landscapes. The mountains and the trail are where I reset, and a big part of why I'm drawn to companies and organizations that exist to get people outside.

I'm also a Head Campus Captain for The Hidden Opponent, a national mental health organization for student athletes, a Treasurer for the Graduate Student Association, and an Instructional Assistant in Statistics and Data Science at Cal Poly.
